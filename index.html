Alien Invasion

by: Sora Ogawa

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I made this game because I saw the demo with the projectiles that aim at the mouse and had the idea to make something like League Invaders but with a slightly different way to play.

2. What I Learned

I learned that I should explain my game with less words since the game instructions looked like it was a lot. I shortened the game instructions so it looked a lot easier to read (it also allowed the player to learn the game's mechanics on their own).

3. Architecture of My Game

The player can shoot bullets by clicking, and enemies die when they touch a bullet. The 3 types of enemies (Enemy, fastEnemy, and weirdEnemy) all move towards the middle of the screen (where the player is).

4. Technical Implementation

Making the enemy movement was something that was difficult. In other projects I coded, movement was usually in one direction, but I had to code the enemies in this game to move towards a certain point. This meant that I couldn't just code the enemies to move one way. At first, I made code that checked if the enemy's x and y values were more or less than the x and y of where it was supposed to go, and made it move in whichever direction the point it was supposed to go to was. This didn't work at first since the enemies moved more than 1 pixel. This caused a strange zigzag motion (for example, if the x-value of the enemy was 1, the x-value of the point it wanted to go was 0, and it moved at 2 pixels every frame, it would move to the left because 1>0, then move to the right because its x-value is now less than 0, and repeat this over and over again). I fixed this by not making the point the enemies wanted to go to a single place and instead making it a range of points close to where they were supposed to go.

5. Most Challenging Part

One of the most challenging parts of this project was making ideas and finding sprites. I'm not very good at making my own ideas, so looking at the demos and building off of one of them helped a lot. Finding a good sprite for my game was tough. I wanted it to be pixelated, but too less pixels would make it stand out against the other sprites, and too much would also make it stand out. In the end, I looked at some references and made my own sprites.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I were to continue working on this game, I would add more types of enemies, redraw some of the sprites and make my own background, add more mechanics, and make the player able to move around (with the aliens following the player).
